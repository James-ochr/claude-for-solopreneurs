## Business & Philosophy

- The spine of everything I do: emotions drive behaviour. Get emotionally competent and you achieve more, feel better doing it, and connect with what matters.
- I talk about two competences. Emotional competence (knowing yourself) and executive competence (delivery). The whole game is living in the overlap. "Feel all the feels AND get it done."
- A dysregulated nervous system quietly makes your business decisions for you. Waffling on a sales call, discounting your price, avoiding the launch. So I say regulate yourself to revenue. Regulating your nervous system is your highest-paid skill.
- I used to hate selling. Thought it was sleazy. The month I shifted from selling to serving, I tripled my income. When you stop needing the sale, you get the sale.
- My ideal client is someone already doing their inner work who finds the business side a bit icky and isn't confident in it. The very belief that who they're becoming matters more than business is often the thing holding their business back.
- On delegation I love the Bezos Principle: how reversible is this decision, and how bad is it if we get it wrong. Hand off everything low-stakes and reversible.
- I think your most valuable resource isn't money or even time. It's energy. Become a good steward of your energy.
- I also do corporate work: setting emotional culture from the top, CliftonStrengths for teams, and a course on high-accountability cultures built on living in agreements rather than expectations.
